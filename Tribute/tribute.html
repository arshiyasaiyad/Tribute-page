Tribute to APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and was a prominent figure in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true." - APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. Kalam was awarded numerous awards and honors throughout his life, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Padma Vibhushan, the country's second-highest civilian award. He was also known as the "People's President" due to his humble and down-to-earth nature.

Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to inspire millions of people around the world through his speeches, books, and lectures. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.

Dr.Kalam was a true visionary who believed in the power of education and technology to transform society. He will always be remembered as a role model and inspiration to generations to come.

APJ Abdul Kalam History

Year Event
1931 Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
1954 Kalam graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
1960 Kalam started working on an expandable rocket project at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
1982 Kalam played a crucial role in the successful launch of India's first satellite, Rohini.
1998 Kalam served as the Chief Project Coordinator for India's nuclear tests in Pokhran.
2002 Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India and served from 2002 to 2007.
2015 Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
